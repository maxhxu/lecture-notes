The expectation is the `average/mean' of a random variable.
\begin{definition}[Expectation]
If $X$ is discrete,
\[
    \mathbb{E}[X] := \sum_{x \in \mathbb{R}} x P(X = x)
\]
and if $X$ is continuous
\[
    \mathbb{E}[X] := \int x f(x) \, dx
\]
\end{definition}
Immediately, $\int x f(x) \, dx = \int x \, dF(x)$. Next, we look at the Concentration Inequality. Consider a simpler version: Markov's Inequality.

\begin{simplethm}[Markov's Inequality]
    If $X$, a random variable, is non-negative ($X \geq 0$), and $\mathbb{E}[X] < \infty$, then we have
    \[
    \forall \epsilon > 0, P(X \geq \epsilon) \leq \frac{\mathbb{E}[x]}{\epsilon}
    \]
\end{simplethm}

\begin{proof}
    Let $Z = \epsilon \cdot I(X \geq \epsilon)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{simplethm}[Chebyshev's Inequality]
    Have $Y$ be a random variable, with $\mathbb{E}[Y^2] < \infty$. Then
\[
    P(|Y - \mathbb{E}[Y]| \geq a) \leq \frac{\mathbb{E}[Y - \mathbb{E}[Y]]^2}{a^2}
\]
\end{simplethm}
